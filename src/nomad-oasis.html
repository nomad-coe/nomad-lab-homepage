Features
What is NOMAD Oasis?
NOMAD Oasis let's you create your own NOMAD. Our public service and Oasis are based on the same software. With NOMAD Oasis you get all the NOMAD features while implementing your specific data-management needs and individual policies. You get an instant overview of all your group's data and can collaborate without compromising privacy.

Use cases
Why use NOMAD Oasis?
There are good reasons to manage your data locally, but with Oasis you are also prepared to publish and share your data via NOMAD.
Privacy
With Oasis you control your data to implement your data policies.
  • Manage data without publishing.
  • Run in your network/VPN.
  • Connect to your user management.
  • Only allow a whitelist of users.
Many data
With Oasis you use your resources and manage as many data as you want.
  • Use your existing storage solution.
  • Simple file-base backup.
  • Only your resources are the limited.
  • Publish selected data to NOMAD.
Custom needs
Oasis can be extended and customized beyond the public NOMAD service.
  • Customized processing and ELNs.
  • Add support for your file formats.
  • Add your own NORTH tools.
  • Use APIs to integrate your lab.

Installation
How to run NOMAD Oasis?
NOMAD is containerized. You can run it on a single computer with docker compose or on a large cluster with kubernetes. It can work for a small group as well as a large research institute.
1. Choose your hardware
NOMAD is a framework that processes your files and allows you to run analysis. The hardware needed depends on how you will use it. Start with any linux box you have and scale-up based on your demands.
2. Configure your Oasis
You can start with easy-to-setup default installation and use all build in tools. Integrate existing storage solutions, connect to your existing user-management, develop schemas and parsers. Participate in NOMAD's ecosystem and share your tools with the community.
3. Easy to back-up
NOMAD is based on files. All data is organized in a regular file system that can be easily integrated in your existing storage and back-up solutions. On top you can export your projects or publish them on the public NOMAD.
